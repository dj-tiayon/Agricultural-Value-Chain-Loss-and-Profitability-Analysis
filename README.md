# 🌾Agricultural-Value-Chain-Loss-and-Profitability-Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes agricultural production, logistics, and sales data to uncover inefficiencies, revenue losses, and profitability challenges across the agricultural value chain using Excel and Power BI.

The analysis evaluates operational performance across crops, farmers, and regions to identify key drivers of revenue loss, assess supply chain efficiency, and generate data-driven insights that support improved profitability, reduced waste, and better strategic decision-making.

The dashboard was designed to help stakeholders:

- Identify major sources of revenue loss
- Understand regional and operational inefficiencies
- Evaluate profitability across crops and regions
- Discover opportunities to improve efficiency and reduce waste

The project combines business analysis, KPI development, and interactive data visualization to transform raw operational data into actionable business insights

## 🚨 Business Problem

Agricultural supply chains often experience significant losses due to spoilage, poor storage conditions, inefficient handling, and transportation challenges. 
These inefficiencies reduce profitability, increase operational costs, and limit the scalability of agricultural operations.

To better understand these challenges and improve decision-making, the project aims to answer the following key business questions:

1. Which crops generate the highest profit margins across regions?
2. Which regions experience the highest transportation losses?
3. What is the average logistics cost per kilogram sold?
4. How much revenue is lost due to spoilage and transport inefficiencies?
5. Which farmer groups or markets operate most efficiently?

##  🎯 Project Objectives

- Identify where revenue losses occur across the agricultural value chain
- Determine the major drivers of losses, particularly spoilage and transport inefficiencies
- Analyze profitability across crops, farmers, and regions
- Evaluate operational efficiency using key metrics such as profit margin and storage utilization
- Provide data-driven recommendations to reduce losses and improve overall profitability and supply chain performance

## 🔍 Key Insights

#### 1. High Revenue Loss Across the Value Chain
Approximately **35.6% of total revenue is lost**, representing a major operational inefficiency that negatively impacts profitability and scalability.

#### 2. Spoilage is the Main Driver of Losses
About **92.99% of total revenue loss (₦429.8M)** is caused by spoilage, significantly outweighing transport-related losses.

#### 3. Central & West Regions Experience the Highest Losses
The **Central (₦102M) and West (₦101M)** regions experience the highest revenue losses, making them priority areas for operational intervention.

#### 4. Strong Profitability Despite Operational Inefficiencies
Profit margins exceed **86% across crops and regions**, indicating strong profitability potential if operational losses are reduced.

## 🚀 Recommendations and Implemantation Plan

| Recommendation | Timeline | Key Steps | Expected Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| Improve post-harvest handling practices | 0–3 Months | Train farmers on better drying, sorting, packaging, and storage techniques | Reduced spoilage losses and improved product quality |
| Prioritize interventions in Central & West regions | 0–3 Months | Deploy targeted operational improvements and monitor regional loss trends | Significant reduction in regional revenue losses |
| Improve storage efficiency and inventory management | 3–6 Months | Introduce inventory tracking and reduce storage duration | Lower spoilage rates and better stock management |
| Scale best practices from top-performing farmers | 6–12 Months | Identify and replicate successful farming methods across the network | Increased profitability and operational efficiency |

> The implementation plan prioritizes spoilage reduction, operational efficiency, and the scaling of best practices to improve long-term profitability.
